UFO SIGHTINGS
Unveiling the Mystery

Exploring Patterns and Associations in UFO Sightings Data

Geographic Distribution & Shapes

We conducted a comprehensive geospatial analysis of UFO sightings to identify regional hotspots and clustering patterns. By visualizing reporting density against population centers, we observed a strong correlation between urbanization and sighting frequency, particularly in North America and Europe. This suggests that while sightings are a global phenomenon, the reporting infrastructure and cultural propensity to report significantly bias the data towards Western nations.

Figure 1: Global distribution of UFO sightings.

Focus on the United States

Refining our analysis to the United States reveal distinct clusters along coastal regions and major metropolitan hubs. Los Angeles and New York emerge as primary hotspots, which aligns with higher population density. However, the data also hints at a "Hollywood Effect," where proximity to media production centers correlates with a higher volume of reports, potentially indicating a cultural influence on perception and reporting.

Figure 2: Detailed US sightings map.

Reported Shapes

"Light" is the most common shape, followed by Triangle, Circle, and Fireball.

Figure 3: Frequency of reported shapes.

Temporal Patterns

Our longitudinal analysis from 1940 to 2020 highlights a dramatic exponential increase in sightings coinciding with the digital age. The spike post-1990 strongly correlates with the widespread adoption of the internet, which democratized the reporting process. Furthermore, diurnal analysis confirms that over 80% of sightings occur during nocturnal hours, specifically between 8 PM and midnight, consistent with visibility conditions favorable for observing aerial anomalies.

Figure 4: Historical trend (1940-2020).
Figure 5: Sightings by time of day.

Big Tech Stock Prices
Tech Titans Analysis

An analysis of 14 Big Tech stocks from 2010 - 2022

Price Changes Over Time

This time-series analysis examines the price trajectories of 14 major technology stocks over a 12-year period. The data reveals a secular bull market across the sector, punctuated by localized volatility events. Most notably, the "V-shaped" recovery following the March 2020 COVID-19 crash demonstrates the sector's resilience and the market's rapid pricing of digital transformation acceleration.

Figure 1: Closing prices (2010-2022).

Trading Volume

Apple (AAPL) was the most actively traded stock, with volume roughly 150% higher than competitors, highlighting its liquidity.

Figure 2: Average daily trading volume.

Investment Strategy

To evaluate potential trading strategies, we backtested a dual Moving Average Crossover system involving the 50-day and 200-day averages. The analysis identified multiple high-confidence "Golden Cross" buy signals that preceded sustained rallies. Using the 50-day DMA as a dynamic support level proved more effective for trend-following in this high-growth sector than static resistance levels.

Conclusion: The tech sector offered immense wealth generation. While timing is hard, technical indicators and diversification (mixing stable dividends like IBM with growth like TSLA) are key.

Cybersecurity Trends
Operational Analysis

Forecasting Security Outcomes from 20 Years of Data

Incident Trends Over Time

Analyzing a dataset of over 500,000 security logs, we reconstructed the historical trend of cyber incidents over two decades. The results show a clear, monotonic increase in attack volume, with significant inflection points mirroring the release of major vulnerabilities (e.g., EternalBlue). This escalating trajectory underscores the growing automated nature of modern cyber threats.

Figure 1: Yearly trend of reported cybersecurity incidents.

Attack Vectors Analysis

We categorized incidents by attack vector to determine the most prevalent threat types. Phishing emerged as the overwhelming leader, accounting for a significant plurality of breaches, followed by Malware injection and DDoS attacks. This distribution suggests that despite technical defenses, social engineering remains the most critical vulnerability in organizational security postures.

Figure 2: Frequency variation by attack type.

Threat Intensity Heatmap

Temporal analysis indicates that malicious activity peaks during specific operational hours, suggesting automated or time-zone specific attack patterns.

Figure 3: Heatmap of threat intensity by time of day.

Predictive Modeling
Safety Operations

Identifying Risk Patterns in Transportation Datasets

Risk Correlation Analysis

Using transportation sensor data, we performed a regression analysis to determine the primary drivers of accident risk. We found a statistically significant positive correlation between speed variance (not just absolute speed) and the safety risk score. This finding validates the "Speed Harmonization" theory, suggesting that stabilizing traffic flow is more critical for safety than simply lowering speed limits.

Figure 1: Correlation between speed variance and risk score.

Model Feature Importance

To construct a predictive risk model, we evaluated feature importance using a Random Forest algorithm. 'Speed Variance', 'Adverse Weather Conditions', and 'Traffic Volume' emerged as the top three predictors. Interestingly, 'Road Type' had a lower predictive power than dynamic environmental factors, indicating that real-time monitoring is essential for accurate risk assessment.

Figure 2: Relative importance of features in the predictive model.

Model Performance

The classification model achieved 85% accuracy in distinguishing between high-risk and low-risk operational states.

Figure 3: Confusion matrix showing model prediction accuracy.
